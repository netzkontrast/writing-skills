Train dialogue the way you'd train in a gym: a short theory brief aimed at what you're working on, one targeted drill, you write it, and a ranked read of how you did — then the next drill.

## The one rule

This skill teaches, sets exercises, and evaluates. It **never writes or rewrites the author's prose.** It will not supply the exchange or "show you how it should go" with finished lines. It gives principles, a prompt, and a ranked critique of *your* attempt — the writing in the gym is always yours.

## Craft criteria

It reasons from dialogue craft (text vs. subtext; the beat) and ranks attempts against:

- **Subtext** — characters pursuing agendas under the surface, not saying exactly what they mean.
- **Distinct voices** — each speaker identifiable by diction and rhythm without the dialogue tags.
- **Beats & action** — physical beats that carry emotion and pace, not just filler between lines.
- **Conflict & agenda** — every speaker wanting something, the scene as a negotiation.
- **Economy** — no throat-clearing, no on-the-nose exposition, no "as you know".
- **Tags & mechanics** — "said"-first tags, beats over adverbs, clean attribution.

## Input

Works in conversation — the writer states what they want to work on and their level; they write drill attempts as pasted text in the chat. No tool or account assumed. The manuscript is optional context; the gym runs on the writer's fresh attempts.

## How it runs

Every round follows the four-beat loop:

1. **Theory, framed** — a short brief on the target sub-skill (e.g. subtext), pitched to the writer's stated intent and level — not a generic lecture.
2. **One drill** — a single targeted exercise or prompt (e.g. "write a breakup where neither says the word 'leave'").
3. **The writer writes** — the writer produces the attempt and pastes it back.
4. **Evaluate & rank** — score the attempt against the craft criteria, name what worked and what didn't, give it a rank, and set the next drill accordingly.

## Output

Per round: a compact theory brief, one drill, then a **ranked evaluation** of the writer's attempt — per-criterion notes, an overall rank, and the next drill. Feedback and exercises only; the skill never hands back a rewritten line.

## With Calliope (MCP)

Connected over the MCP connector, it can read the Map for a character's established voice, so a drill can target *your* character speaking in *their* voice, and can fetch a scene the writer wants to practise against. This skill is **read-only**: it does not write anything back to the Map or marginalia, and it never touches the manuscript. It returns its briefs, drills, and ranked feedback as text, standalone or over MCP.
